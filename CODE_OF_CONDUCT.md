# Code of Conduct

## ü§ù Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## ‚≠ê Our Standards

### Positive Behavior ‚úÖ

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Being Respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being Collaborative**: Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- **Being Supportive**: Showing empathy towards other community members
- **Being Professional**: Focusing on what is best for the community
- **Being Open-Minded**: Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- **Being Gracious**: Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- **Being Responsible**: Taking accountability for mistakes and learning from them

### Unacceptable Behavior ‚ùå

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- **Harassment**: Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory jokes, language, or behavior
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Sexual Content**: Sexual language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- **Intimidation**: Threatening behavior or sustained disruption of discussions
- **Spam**: Unsolicited advertising, promotion, or off-topic content
- **Plagiarism**: Claiming others' work as your own
- **Impersonation**: Pretending to be another person or organization
- **Other**: Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## üëÆ Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## üåç Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- **GitHub Repository**: Issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews
- **Communication Channels**: Discord, Telegram, Twitter, email
- **Events**: Conferences, meetups, hackathons (virtual or in-person)
- **Official Representation**: When representing the project in public spaces

## üö® Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any concerns:

1. **Report Privately**
   - Open a private GitHub issue with [CONDUCT] tag
   - Email the maintainers (if email provided)
   - Contact via Discord DM to moderators

2. **What to Include**
   - Your contact information
   - Description of the incident
   - Any supporting evidence (links, screenshots)
   - Names of any witnesses
   - Whether you want to be anonymous

3. **What to Expect**
   - **Acknowledgment**: Within 48 hours
   - **Investigation**: Confidential review of the incident
   - **Action**: Appropriate response based on severity
   - **Follow-up**: Update on the outcome (if appropriate)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. We will protect the identity of the reporter unless disclosure is required by law or explicit permission is given.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining consequences:

### 1. Correction üìù

**Community Impact**: Minor inappropriate behavior or language.

**Consequence**: 
- Private, written warning
- Clarification of why the behavior was inappropriate
- Request for public apology (optional)

### 2. Warning ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Community Impact**: Violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**:
- Written warning with consequences for continued behavior
- No interaction with involved parties for a specified period
- Violation of terms may lead to temporary or permanent ban

### 3. Temporary Ban üö´

**Community Impact**: Serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**:
- Temporary ban from community interaction and communication
- No public or private interaction with community members
- Duration: Specified period (typically 30-90 days)
- Violations may lead to permanent ban

### 4. Permanent Ban üî¥

**Community Impact**: Pattern of violations, sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence**:
- Permanent ban from all community interaction
- No public or private interaction allowed
- May include reporting to relevant platforms (GitHub, etc.)

## ü§î Questions & Clarifications

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I disagree with a technical decision?**  
A: Technical disagreements are welcome! Present your case respectfully with reasoning and evidence. Maintainers have final say but value community input.

**Q: Can I use humor or memes?**  
A: Yes, when appropriate and not offensive. Keep it professional and inclusive.

**Q: What if I accidentally violate the code of conduct?**  
A: We all make mistakes. If brought to your attention, apologize and commit to doing better. Good faith efforts are appreciated.

**Q: How do I report a maintainer's behavior?**  
A: Follow the same reporting process. If uncomfortable reporting to maintainers, contact GitHub support directly.

**Q: What if I disagree with an enforcement decision?**  
A: You may appeal by providing additional context or information to the moderators within 7 days of the decision.

## üåü Positive Community Building

### How to Contribute Positively

1. **Welcome Newcomers**: Help onboard new contributors with patience
2. **Share Knowledge**: Document learnings and help others grow
3. **Give Credit**: Acknowledge others' contributions and ideas
4. **Be Patient**: Remember everyone has different skill levels and backgrounds
5. **Assume Good Intent**: Give people the benefit of the doubt
6. **Celebrate Success**: Recognize and appreciate contributions
7. **Mentor Others**: Share your expertise to help others learn

### Building Inclusive Culture

We strive to:
- Use inclusive language (they/them when gender unknown)
- Provide multiple ways to contribute (code, docs, testing, design)
- Welcome contributions from all skill levels
- Respect different time zones and availability
- Make discussions accessible (avoid jargon, explain concepts)
